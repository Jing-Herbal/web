There's a moment most people have when they first get serious about their health. You start reading labels. You start asking questions. You start noticing that the supplement industry — a market worth more than $60 billion globally — is almost entirely built on blind trust.

Vague labelling. Paid anecdotal stories. A founder who spent time in the mountains of China. A claim that references a 3,000-year tradition without a single link to a peer-reviewed study.

We had that moment. And instead of gatekeeping the information we discovered, we built a platform for you — the uncompromising optimiser that cares deeply about what goes into their body.

The problem

The supplement industry has a transparency problem.

And it runs deep.

Most companies and influencers will tell you what a herb does. Very few will tell you how it does it. Fewer still will tell you how well the evidence actually supports that claim — and almost none will tell you when it doesn't.

The result is the opportunity cost of investing time and money into a protocol that is suboptimal, and a market flooded with products that are at their best ineffective and at their worst dangerous.

"Products where 'clinically studied' means a single unpublished pilot study. Where you're paying $80 a month for something that may be no more potent than dried mushroom powder you could buy from a grocery store."

We're not interested in selling you a product. We're interested in helping you reach your goals.

What Jing is

A science-backed TCM formulary — built for people who read the study before they buy.

Jing is a science-backed Traditional Chinese Medicine formulary. We review the clinical evidence for TCM herbs, rate it honestly, so you can make the most informed decision on your health.

Our platform currently covers 49 herbs — each with a full clinical dossier that includes:

  • The active compounds and what they do at the molecular level
  • A rated evidence summary based on RCT quality and quantity
  • The mechanism of action, explained in plain language
  • Dosage guidance drawn directly from peer-reviewed studies
  • A complete safety profile, including drug interactions and contraindications

We publish the evidence whether it supports the herb or not. If a study weakens the case for a herb we evaluate, we update the rating. If a meta-analysis changes the picture — even if it's unflattering — we share the data.

We built the education platform first because we believe an informed consumer is a better one. If you understand why lion's mane 猴头菇 works, you'll take it correctly, you'll notice the effects, and you'll come back. The dossiers exist to close that gap.

Why traditional Chinese medicine

Three millennia of pharmacology. Now with peer-reviewed evidence.

Traditional Chinese medicine has been practised for over three millennia. That's not a marketing claim — it's a fact about the depth and duration of a pharmacological tradition that predates Western medicine by two thousand years.

But it's also not a reason to take anything on faith.

The interesting thing happening in medicine right now is that the tools of modern pharmacology are being applied to traditional systems for the first time at scale. Randomised controlled trials. HPLC standardisation. Pharmacokinetic studies. And what's emerging is something remarkable: many of the herbs that ancient physicians classified as superior tonics genuinely hold up under scrutiny.

These are not folk remedies. They're pharmacologically active compounds with growing clinical evidence behind them.

The problem was never with the herbs. The problem was the lack of demand for transparency and critical thinking applied to the products we put into our bodies.

Why now

Two things are converging.

The first is cultural. A generation of men and women are taking their biology seriously in ways that weren't socially acceptable five years ago. Testosterone, cortisol, DHT, sleep quality, skin, jawline density — these are no longer fringe topics. They're mainstream. The biohacking community has mainstreamed the idea that your phenotype is an input, not a fixed output.

The second is scientific. The tools of modern clinical research are being applied to TCM at scale for the first time. There are now thousands of peer-reviewed studies on medicinal mushrooms and adaptogenic herbs. The evidence base is growing faster than the supplement industry has been able to respond to it.

The opportunity is a platform that bridges both — that takes TCM seriously as a pharmacological tradition, applies modern evidence standards to it, and delivers the results to a market that is finally ready to engage with both.

A note on transparency

We're not doctors. And we'll always say so.

We've spent a year in the literature. We present the clinical data. We explain the mechanisms. We link to the studies. We tell you what the evidence says and how strong it is. What we don't do is make claims we can't substantiate, overstate the certainty of preliminary findings, or pretend that correlation is causation.

If you're managing a health condition, talk to a doctor. If you're on medication, check the interactions in our database before adding any herb. If the evidence for something is weak, we'll say so.

This is what we mean by modern proof.
